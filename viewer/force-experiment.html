What to compare

• Core/periphery hierarchy — does one algorithm push hub nodes to the middle and leaves to the edge, or does it flatten everything into a blob?

• Cluster separation — do related artifacts (e.g., all USB-class artifacts) cluster visibly, or do they scatter?

• Edge crossings / spaghetti — count visible crossings at a quick glance. Lower = better readability.

• Label crowding — fewer overlapping labels at the same zoom = better layout.

ForceAtlas2 knobs

• LinLog: logarithmic attraction — compresses dense clusters so outer structure is visible. The single biggest spaghetti-reduction lever.

• Strong gravity: keeps loosely-connected nodes from drifting off-canvas. On by default for dense graphs.

• Dissuade hubs: repels hubs from each other — good when your hubs are mutually-exclusive (substrate classes). Try toggling it for your corpus.

• Gravity / scaling / iterations: fine-tuning. Defaults land at 400 iterations, gravity 1, scaling 10.
